📸 Getting an analysable photo
  1. Set the bike on a trainer, rollers, or have a friend hold it upright
  2. Camera positioned exactly 90° to the side, level with the bike's centre, 3–4 m back
  3. Pedal at the 3 o'clock crank position (forward crank arm horizontal) if possible
  4. Full bike and rider must be visible in frame — wheels to helmet
  5. Wear fitted clothing so joint lines are visible
  6. Good even lighting, no strong shadows
💡 The camera should be roughly hip height and completely level — a tilted phone will skew every angle estimate.

Body measurements

Core dimensions
📏 How to measure height
  1. Stand barefoot on a hard floor, heels and back against a wall
  2. Look straight ahead, chin parallel to the floor
  3. Rest a flat book on your head, spine pressed to wall
  4. Mark the wall at the book's lower edge
  5. Measure from floor to the mark in centimetres
💡 Measure in the morning — you can be 1–2 cm taller before your spine compresses.
⚖️ How to measure weight
  1. Use a digital scale on a firm, flat floor
  2. Weigh first thing in the morning, after the bathroom
  3. Wear minimal clothing
💡 Rider weight affects saddle pressure and centre of gravity calculations.
Leg length (inseam) — most critical
📐 How to measure inseam
  1. Stand barefoot on hard floor, feet 15–20 cm apart
  2. Place a hardcover book between your legs, spine facing up
  3. Press the book firmly upward into your crotch (simulate sitting on a saddle)
  4. While a friend holds the book level, mark the wall at the top of the spine
  5. Measure from floor to mark in centimetres
💡 The single most important measurement for saddle height. Repeat 2–3 times and average.
Reach measurements
💪 How to measure arm length
  1. Stand upright, arms relaxed at your sides
  2. Find your acromion — the bony bump at the top of your shoulder
  3. Measure from the acromion straight down to the centre of your wrist crease
  4. Keep arm straight but not locked
💡 Measure both arms and use the longer one.
🧍 How to measure torso length
  1. Sit upright on a hard chair, back straight and tall
  2. Tilt your head forward — feel for C7 vertebra at the base of your neck
  3. Measure from C7 straight down to the seat surface
💡 Drives handlebar reach and stem length calculations.
🤸 How to assess flexibility
  1. Stand barefoot, feet together, legs completely straight
  2. Slowly bend forward, reach down as far as comfortable
  3. Note where your fingertips reach
💡 Poor hip flexor flexibility is the most common cause of lower back pain on the bike.
Rider history
🔍 Common pain → cause patterns
  1. Front knee → saddle too low or too far forward
  2. Rear knee → saddle too high
  3. Lower back → excessive reach or tight hamstrings
  4. Neck/shoulder → too much weight on hands
  5. Wrist → over-extended reach, no elbow bend
  6. Foot/heel → cleat misaligned, saddle too high
  7. Numbness → saddle too high or nose angled up

Bike details

Bike setup
🚲 Finding your frame size
  1. Check the sticker/engraving on the seat tube
  2. Road: usually in cm (52, 54, 56, 58)
  3. MTB/hybrid: usually XS/S/M/L/XL
  4. If unknown, measure BB centre to top of seat tube
Components
🔍 Finding your wheel size
  1. Look at the sidewall of either tyre
  2. Size is printed there — e.g. 700×25c or 29×2.2
  3. Road: almost always 700c
🔩 Finding crank length
  1. Look at the back face of either crank arm
  2. Length is stamped there (170, 172.5, 175)
  3. If unclear: measure BB centre to pedal hole centre
💡 Riders under 168 cm often benefit from 165–170 mm cranks.
🪑 Identifying saddle type
  1. Flat: minimal padding, narrow, slight curve
  2. Padded: thick foam/gel, wider
  3. Cut-out: central hole/channel
  4. Noseless/ISM: very short or no nose
🏁 Handlebar types
  1. Drop: curves downward — road/gravel
  2. Flat: straight bar — hybrid/MTB
  3. Riser: angles up toward rider
  4. Bullhorn: forward extensions
  5. Aero/TT: elbow pads + extensions

Current setup (optional — improves accuracy)
📏 Measuring saddle height
  1. Bike on trainer or leaned upright on level ground
  2. Find bottom bracket centre (axle in middle of cranks)
  3. Measure straight up to saddle top, along seat tube line
📏 Measuring saddle setback
  1. Hang a plumb line from the nose of the saddle
  2. Measure horizontal distance to bottom bracket centre
  3. Most riders sit 20–50 mm behind BB
📏 Finding stem length
  1. Look at the side of the stem
  2. Length is stamped there (80–110 mm)
  3. Or measure steerer clamp centre to bar clamp centre
💡 Every 10 mm of stem length changes reach by ~8–9 mm.
